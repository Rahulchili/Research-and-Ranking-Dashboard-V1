1. Executive Summary
Rahul's dashboard surfaces a Low / Medium / High management-guidance confidence label intended to summarize how reliably management has delivered on what it told investors. The v1.0 implementation contains a denominator error: it appears to assume that an 8-quarter window yields 8 verifiable forward-guidance outcomes, when in fact it yields only 7. The 8th quarter's forward guidance points to a quarter that has not yet been reported, so it cannot be scored.
The fix in one sentence: For an n-quarter analysis window, exactly n minus 1 guidance-to-actual pairs are closed and scorable; the most recent forward guidance is pending and must be excluded from the confidence denominator.
This v2.0 PRD preserves Rahul's original correction and goes further. It hardens the scoring methodology so the resulting Low / Medium / High label is statistically defensible, auditable, and useful to Megha's research team. The key enhancements:
Generalized formula. Replaces the hardcoded 8 with a generic n; closed_pairs(n) = max(0, n − 1), gated on n ≥ 4 for the label to be reportable.
Magnitude-aware claim scoring. Replaces the coarse 1.00 / 0.50 / 0.00 buckets with a continuous score that distinguishes a narrow miss from a wide one, while preserving the v1.0 buckets as the default discretization for analyst readability.
Claim weighting by materiality. Revenue, EPS, and gross margin carry higher weight than capex, tax rate, or one-off items — preventing a small line-item miss from dragging an otherwise strong quarter.
Explicit tolerance bands. Defines what "in-line" and "slight miss" mean numerically, so two analysts scoring the same claim arrive at the same number.
Withdrawn / revised guidance handling. Withdrawn guidance is a distinct outcome (not the same as a miss and not the same as pending).
Statistical context. Reports the standard error and a 95% confidence band alongside the point estimate — because n = 7 is statistically thin and analysts should see the uncertainty.
Sandbagging signal. A separate diagnostic that flags chronic, asymmetric beats — a signal that management may be setting deliberately low bars.
Operational vs strategic split. Reports two sub-scores so analysts can see whether management hits the numbers but misses the strategic milestones, or vice versa.
Hardened edge cases. Specifies behavior for missing transcripts, missing actuals, zero measurable claims, and division-by-zero risks in the trend calculation.
Out of scope for v2.0
This PRD touches only the confidence-scoring layer. The broader investment ranking, sector weights, options analysis, technical analysis, and valuation modules are intentionally untouched. They are listed under Section 16 (Future Enhancements) for sequencing.
2. Background and Problem Statement
2.1 Where We Are Today
The current dashboard pulls in eight quarters of earnings calls and SEC filings per ticker. It extracts management's forward-looking claims, compares them against actuals, and emits a Low / Medium / High confidence label. The intent is correct; the arithmetic is not.
2.2 The Denominator Error
Across multiple ticker dashboards, the confidence card appears to be computed against a denominator larger than the number of pairs that can actually be verified. In the worst case, the most recent quarter's forward guidance is being scored as if its target quarter had already reported — which it has not.
Why this matters
Three downstream risks: (1) the Low/Medium/High label can be off by one band; (2) pending guidance can be silently treated as delivered or missed, distorting management credibility; (3) Megha's analysts may anchor on a credibility signal that is more confident than the evidence supports.
2.3 The Correct Mental Model
Forward guidance issued in quarter Q can only be evaluated when quarter Q+1 has reported. Therefore in an n-quarter window:
closed_pairs    = n - 1     (one pair per quarter except the last)
pending_pairs   = 1         (the last quarter's forward guidance)
total_pairs     = n         (closed + pending)
denominator     = closed_pairs   ← this is what the score uses
3. Objectives and Non-Goals
3.1 Objectives
Compute the confidence score using only closed (verified) guidance-to-actual pairs.
Display the pending pair separately, never folded into the score.
Make the methodology explainable to a buy-side analyst in under 30 seconds.
Make the methodology defensible — auditable down to the specific claim, quote, and outcome.
Report uncertainty (SE, CI) alongside the point estimate so analysts can calibrate trust.
Separate operational delivery (numbers) from strategic delivery (milestones).
Detect and flag sandbagging behavior.
Behave gracefully when data is missing — never silently degrade.
3.2 Non-Goals (v2.0)
Do not change the broader investment ranking or sector weighting.
Do not modify options, technical, or valuation analysis modules.
Do not introduce cross-company benchmarking yet (deferred to v3).
Do not score qualitative management commentary that lacks a measurable target.
Do not attempt to score multi-year strategic claims that have not yet matured.
4. Glossary and Definitions
5. Conceptual Model
5.1 The n → n−1 Rule
For an n-quarter analysis window with all actuals reported through quarter n, exactly n−1 forward-guidance pairs are closed. This is the single most important invariant in the system.
The pairing table for the current default (n = 8):
 
5.2 Minimum Viable Window
The label is suppressed (shown as "Insufficient Data") when fewer than four closed pairs exist. Below this floor the noise dominates the signal and any Low/Medium/High label is misleading.
Decision
Floor: n ≥ 4 quarters of actuals → at least 3 closed pairs → minimum viable label. Below the floor, render "Insufficient Data — N closed pairs" and skip color coding.
5.3 Why Not Roll the Pending Pair Into Score? (NEW)
Two reasons. First, partial-period information (a few weeks into the target quarter, the company has typically not preannounced) is not enough to assign a delivery score. Second, if the dashboard scored pending pairs as half-credit or as the in-progress consensus, it would manufacture confidence the underlying evidence does not support. The cleaner rule is binary: closed = scored, pending = excluded.
6. Scoring Methodology
6.1 Claim-Level Delivery Score
Each measurable claim receives a delivery score on a 0.00 to 1.00 scale. v1.0 used three discrete buckets; v2.0 keeps those buckets as the default analyst-readable scoring but adds an optional magnitude-aware continuous score for audit precision.
6.1.1 Discrete Scoring (Default Display)
6.1.2 Continuous Scoring (Audit View, NEW)
For analysts who want precision, the dashboard exposes an optional continuous score. The formula maps the deviation between the actual and the guided range into the [0.00, 1.00] interval, with an asymmetric kink at the range boundary.
Let:
  L = guided low end
  H = guided high end
  M = guided midpoint = (L + H) / 2
  A = actual outcome
  T = tolerance band (default 2% of M for point guidance,
                      0 for explicit ranges)
 
Effective low  = L - T
Effective high = H + T   (favorable direction only)
 
If A is in [Effective low, Effective high]:
    continuous_score = 1.00
Elif A < Effective low:
    shortfall_pct = (Effective low - A) / max(|M|, 1e-9)
    continuous_score = max(0.00, 1.00 - 10 * shortfall_pct)
    // 5% miss → 0.50; 10% miss → 0.00
Else:  // A > Effective high in the favorable direction
    continuous_score = 1.00   // Beats are capped at 1.00 for scoring
    // Beat magnitude is tracked separately for the sandbagging signal
Why beats are capped at 1.00
Otherwise companies that sandbag (guide low so they always beat) would appear maximally credible. The beat magnitude is informative but it belongs in the sandbagging diagnostic (Section 8.4), not the credibility score.
6.2 Claim Weighting by Materiality (NEW)
Not all guidance is equally important. A 50bps tax-rate miss should not weigh the same as a 5% revenue miss. v2.0 introduces explicit claim weights:
The weights are deliberately conservative and round to half-points so they are easy to override per sector. Sector-specific weight overrides are out of scope for v2.0 but the data model supports them.
6.3 Quarter-Pair Score
A quarter-pair score is the weighted average of the claim-level scores for that pair:
pair_score(pair) =
    Σ (weight_i × delivery_score_i)  for closed claims in pair
    ──────────────────────────────
              Σ weight_i
 
Minimum claim threshold (NEW):
    If the pair has fewer than 2 measurable, scorable claims,
    mark it as "Insufficient Data" and exclude from the
    confidence score denominator.
Why a minimum claim threshold
A quarter where management gave only one measurable claim (e.g., revenue only, no margin or EPS guidance) is not a robust signal. Scoring it equally to a quarter with five claims overweights coincidence. The pair is still displayed in the audit table — just marked "Insufficient Data" and skipped in the average.
6.4 Overall Management Confidence Score
The Management Confidence Score (MCS) is the simple average of the scorable closed pair scores:
mcs =  Σ pair_score(p)  for p in scorable_closed_pairs
       ──────────────────────────────────────────────
                  count(scorable_closed_pairs)
 
confidence_score_pct = round(mcs * 100, 1)
6.4.1 Recency-Weighted Variant (NEW, Optional)
Some analysts argue that recent quarters should weigh more — a company that missed eight quarters ago but has delivered for the last four is structurally different from one trending the other way. The dashboard exposes both the equal-weighted MCS and a recency-weighted MCS for transparency.
Linear-decay weights for n = 7 closed pairs (oldest → newest):
    w = [0.6, 0.8, 1.0, 1.2, 1.4, 1.6, 1.8]
    (normalize so they sum to count)
 
mcs_recency = Σ (w_i × pair_score_i) / Σ w_i
 
Both are displayed:
    MCS (equal-weighted): 87.9%
    MCS (recency-weighted): 89.4%
    Δ = +1.5% → mild improving bias
7. Confidence Levels and Thresholds
7.1 Label Mapping
7.2 Boundary Handling (NEW)
Exact-boundary scores (79.99% vs 80.00%) should not produce flickering labels. Apply standard rounding to one decimal, then compare on rounded value. A score of 79.95% rounds to 80.0% → High. A score of 79.94% rounds to 79.9% → Medium. This is documented in the methodology tooltip.
7.3 Statistical Uncertainty Reporting (NEW)
With n = 7 closed pairs, the confidence band on the MCS point estimate is wide. The dashboard reports:
Inputs: pair_scores = [s_1, s_2, ..., s_k]   (k = closed scorable pairs)
 
mean = MCS
std  = sample standard deviation of pair_scores
SE   = std / sqrt(k)
CI95 = [mean - 1.96 * SE,  mean + 1.96 * SE]   clipped to [0, 1]
 
Displayed as:
    MCS: 87.9%  (95% CI: 79.1% – 96.7%, n = 7)
 
If the CI spans two label bands, also display:
    "Label is sensitive to one or two quarters — interpret with care."
Why this matters
Two companies can both score 82% with very different stability. One might have pair scores [0.80, 0.85, 0.80, 0.85, 0.80, 0.85, 0.80] — rock-solid. The other might have [1.00, 0.50, 1.00, 0.50, 1.00, 0.50, 1.25 capped to 1.00] — equally High, but the variance signal tells the analyst the underlying behavior is choppy. Standard error is how you surface that.
8. Trend, Withdrawn Guidance, and Sandbagging
8.1 Recent Trend
Compares the average of the most recent 3 closed scorable pairs to the full-window MCS:
recent_avg  = mean of latest 3 scorable closed pair scores
overall_avg = MCS
 
if recent_avg >= overall_avg + 0.05:
    trend = "Improving"
elif recent_avg <= overall_avg - 0.05:
    trend = "Deteriorating"
else:
    trend = "Stable"
 
Guard (NEW):
    if count(scorable_closed_pairs) < 3:
        trend = "Insufficient Data"
        (avoids division-by-zero and meaningless single-pair trends)
8.2 Withdrawn Guidance (NEW)
When management formally rescinds guidance (typical events: regulatory pause, supply shock, acquisition close, force majeure), the claim is recorded with status "withdrawn" and a delivery score of 0.25. The audit trail logs the rescission date, the reason given, and the original guidance text.
Why 0.25 and not 0.00
A withdrawal is a planning failure but not necessarily an execution failure — the company may still hit the underlying number; we just won't know. Zero is too harsh; full credit is too lenient. 0.25 is the institutional convention used by sell-side credibility models we benchmarked against.
8.3 Revised Guidance (NEW)
If management revises guidance mid-quarter (e.g., at an investor conference between earnings calls), the most recent revision is the one scored. The audit trail records both versions and the revision date, so an analyst can see whether the company guided down before delivering and inflate-deflated their way to a "beat."
8.4 Sandbagging Diagnostic (NEW)
Independent of the confidence score, the dashboard computes a sandbagging signal — the mean signed beat magnitude across the closed pairs for revenue and EPS:
For each closed claim where category in {revenue, eps}:
    signed_beat_pct = (actual - guided_midpoint) / |guided_midpoint|
 
sandbag_index = mean of signed_beat_pct values
 
Thresholds:
    sandbag_index >= +5.0%  → "Likely sandbagging — chronic large beats"
    sandbag_index in [+1.5%, +5.0%) → "Mild conservatism"
    sandbag_index in (−1.5%, +1.5%) → "Calibrated guidance"
    sandbag_index <= −1.5% → "Optimistic guidance — chronic shortfalls"
 
Display as a small secondary card under the main confidence card.
Interaction with the main score
A sandbagging flag does NOT change the headline MCS. It is a parallel diagnostic that analysts can use to qualify a "High Confidence" reading: a 92% MCS combined with a +6% sandbag index means the company hits guidance because the bar is low, not because operations are heroic.

11. Algorithm Specification
11.1 Function Contract
The implementation must expose one top-level function with this signature. All other helpers are internal.
function computeConfidence(quarterRecords, options = {}) → ConfidenceObject
 
Parameters:
  quarterRecords : Array of QuarterRecord, chronological ascending
  options:
    minClosedPairsForLabel  (default 3)
    minClaimsPerPair        (default 2)
    tolerancePctDefault     (default 0.02)
    recencyWindow           (default 3)
    trendDelta              (default 0.05)
    useRecencyWeighting     (default false; if true, applies linear decay)
    weights                 (override the category weight table)
 
Returns:
  ConfidenceObject (see Section 10.4)
 
Throws:
  Never. All failures surface as data_quality_flags on the output.
11.2 Reference Pseudocode
function computeConfidence(quarterRecords, options = {}) {
  const opts = withDefaults(options);
  const n = quarterRecords.length;
  const flags = [];
 
  // 1. Build pairs
  const pairs = [];
  for (let i = 0; i < n - 1; i++) {
    pairs.push(buildPair(quarterRecords[i], quarterRecords[i + 1]));
  }
  if (n >= 1) pairs.push(buildPendingPair(quarterRecords[n - 1]));
 
  // 2. Score each closed pair
  const closed = pairs.filter(p => p.status === "closed");
  const scorable = closed.filter(
    p => p.scorable_claim_count >= opts.minClaimsPerPair
  );
 
  closed.forEach(p => {
    if (p.scorable_claim_count < opts.minClaimsPerPair) {
      p.status = "insufficient";
      p.pair_score = null;
    } else {
      p.pair_score = weightedAvg(p.claims, opts.weights);
    }
  });
 
  // 3. Compute MCS
  if (scorable.length < opts.minClosedPairsForLabel) {
    return insufficientDataObject(quarterRecords, scorable.length, flags);
  }
  const scores = scorable.map(p => p.pair_score);
  const mcs = mean(scores);
  const stdev = sampleStdDev(scores);
  const se = stdev / Math.sqrt(scores.length);
  const ci95 = [
    clip01(mcs - 1.96 * se),
    clip01(mcs + 1.96 * se)
  ];
 
  // 4. Recency weighting (optional second number)
  const mcsRecency = opts.useRecencyWeighting !== false
    ? recencyWeightedMean(scores)
    : null;
 
  // 5. Label
  const label = labelFromMcs(mcs);
  const labelSensitive = !sameLabel(ci95[0], ci95[1]);
 
  // 6. Trend
  const trend = scores.length >= opts.recencyWindow
    ? trendFromScores(scores, opts.recencyWindow, opts.trendDelta, mcs)
    : "Insufficient Data";
 
  // 7. Sandbag diagnostic
  const sandbag = computeSandbagIndex(scorable);
 
  return assembleConfidenceObject({
    quarterRecords, scorable, mcs, mcsRecency, se, ci95,
    label, labelSensitive, trend, sandbag, flags
  });
}

14. Acceptance Criteria
The refinement is complete only when every box below can be ticked.
14.1 Calculation
Dashboard reads quarterRecords.length dynamically; no hardcoded 8 or 10.
closed_pairs = n − 1 for any n ≥ 2.
Most recent forward guidance is always marked pending.
Pending guidance is always excluded from MCS.
Pairs with fewer than 2 scorable claims are marked Insufficient and excluded.
Withdrawn claims score 0.25 and are logged.
Label is suppressed when scorable_closed_pairs < 3.
Recent trend logic guards against fewer than 3 scorable pairs.
MCS, SE, and 95% CI are all computed and exposed.
Sandbag index is computed for revenue and EPS claims only.
14.2 Display
Confidence card shows label, MCS, CI band, basis, pending indicator, trend, and sandbag posture.
Quarter-pair table shows all n pairs in chronological order.
Pending pair row is visually distinct (italic / muted).
Drill-down view exposes every claim's evidence quote and source citation.
No occurrence of forbidden language anywhere in the rendered UI.
Methodology tooltip explains the n−1 rule in plain English.
14.3 Data Quality
Every scored claim has an evidence_quote, evidence_source, actual, and actual_source.
Missing transcripts and missing actuals each emit a data_quality_flag.
Fiscal quarter labels are used everywhere; calendar dates are tooltips only.
schema_version is present on every persisted object.
computed_at timestamp is updated on every recomputation.

16. Risks, Limitations, and Future Enhancements
16.1 Known Limitations
n = 7 closed pairs is a small sample. The 95% CI will frequently span two label bands. The label_sensitive flag is a partial mitigation, not a fix.
Claim weights are global. A semis-vs-biotech comparison would benefit from sector-calibrated weights, deferred to v3.
"Withdrawn" at 0.25 is a convention, not a derived constant. It can be tuned once we have a larger empirical sample.
Sandbagging is detected only for revenue and EPS. Companies can sandbag on margins or FCF; v3 should expand coverage.
Multi-year strategic claims are excluded. A long-horizon credibility module is a v3 candidate.

18. Closing Note to Rahul
The confidence score is a small piece of the dashboard but it is the piece that signals whether the broader institutional grading is honest. If the denominator is wrong, every label downstream inherits the error. If the methodology is opaque, every Megha-vs-PM conversation about a ticker bogs down in litigating the math.
Build it once, build it auditable, and document it inline so the next engineer (and the next analyst) can read the tooltip and trust what they see. Eight analyzed quarters give us seven verified pairs. The seventh pair carries the weight of the label. The eighth quarter's guidance is a promise, not yet evidence.
Score what is evidenced. Display what is pending. Quantify the uncertainty. Surface the sandbagging. That is the contract this PRD asks you to deliver.

Claim | A single measurable forward-looking statement with a metric, a direction or range, and an implicit or explicit target quarter.
Closed Pair | Guidance-to-actuals pair where the target quarter has reported. Eligible for scoring.
Pending Pair | Guidance-to-actuals pair where the target quarter has not yet reported. Displayed; excluded from score.
Withdrawn Guidance | Guidance that management formally rescinded before the target quarter reported. A distinct outcome from delivered, missed, or pending.
Revised Guidance | Guidance updated by management mid-quarter (e.g., at an investor conference). The most recent version is the one scored; revisions are logged for the audit trail.
In-line | Actual falls within the guided range, or within the tolerance band around a point estimate.
Tolerance Band | Default ±2% of midpoint for point guidance with no range provided. Configurable per metric.
MCS | Management Credibility Score. The detailed per-claim composite, expressed as a percentage.
Confidence Level | The simplified dashboard label: Low / Medium / High. Derived from MCS.
Recent Trend | Direction of MCS over the latest three closed pairs vs the full-window MCS.
Sandbagging | Pattern of guidance set deliberately low so the company can routinely beat. Detected via signed beat magnitude.

Withdrawn guidance | Score = 0.25; logged in audit trail with rescission reason.
Revised guidance mid-quarter | Score the most recent revision. Log both versions.
Calendar vs fiscal quarter confusion | Always use fiscal quarter labels. Tooltip shows the calendar equivalent.
Company changes fiscal calendar | Document the change date in the audit trail. Score continues on the new calendar; do not blend overlapping periods.
No forward guidance at all (e.g., Berkshire) | Confidence score is N/A. Display "Issuer does not provide forward guidance — confidence score not applicable."
Pre-announcement / pre-released results | If the company pre-announced before the target quarter's official report, treat the pre-announced figure as the actual for scoring purposes; flag in the audit trail.

Multi-year guidance | Excluded from the quarter-to-quarter score. May be tracked in a separate long-horizon credibility module (out of scope for v2.0).
